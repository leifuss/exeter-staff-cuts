Professorial pay — Russell Group comparators

Professors get no automatic increments: pay rises only via a discretionary review every two years, only some of which succeed. So the chief cost lever is the award rate, not the structure, and the scales are far more uniform than the lower grades. A professor starts at the band floor at 48 and retires at 67 (~9 reviews); each grants one step with the chosen probability. Band 3 (rare, by exception) is excluded. Kept separate from the non-professorial models. Weighted by Exeter's 510 professors applied to every structure.
