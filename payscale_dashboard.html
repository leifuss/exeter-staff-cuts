Russell Group academic pay — structure & cost

Almost all academic staff in the Russell Group are paid on the same national salary spine, so a given spine point pays the same everywhere up to the national ceiling (point 51); above that, points are institution-specific local extensions. What differs is each university's grade structure: where it draws grade boundaries and how many real increments sit inside each grade. Tab 1 shows the structures; Tab 2 turns them into a cost comparison. Exeter is highlighted throughout.

What this tab covers — and what it doesn't. It shows the three permanent non-professorial academic grades (Lecturer / Senior Lecturer / Associate Professor equivalents). It does not include professorial pay, which is structured differently (discretionary review, not annual increments) and is treated separately in the Professorial and All-in tabs; nor does it cover research-only staff, who sit on a separate grade not modelled here. Staff numbers come from HESA (2024/25), not from the university directly.

About the "where staff sit" setting. We don't know how Exeter's staff are actually distributed within each grade, so the model lets you try three assumptions and see how much it matters: most staff near the bottom of their grade (fast promotion), spread evenly, or most near the top (slow promotion). The pound figures shift, but the ranking of structures stays the same under all three.

Assumptions & limits (please read before quoting figures)
• Cost view applies Exeter's 2024/25 HESA workforce (E&R, or E&R + Education-only via the selector; R-only fixed-term postdocs excluded) to every structure; Senior Lecturer and Associate Professor are split 50/50 into Grades 2 & 3 (HESA combines them; Exeter's true split would move the figures, and Grade 3 is the main driver).
• Contribution points are excluded from cost; since Exeter's grades are short, this may understate Exeter.
• Salaries are the verified 2025-26 national spine; +2% for 2026-27 is UCEA's provisional offer.
• On-costs: employer NI 15% above £5,000, USS pension 14.5%, apprenticeship levy 0.5%.
• Structural comparison only — not a budget forecast; excludes professorial pay, allowances, transition.
• Source data: grades.csv, national_spine.csv, cost_model_results.csv.
