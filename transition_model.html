Transition savings — phasing in a cheaper structure, no pay cuts

If Exeter kept everyone's current salary but moved its non-professorial grade structure to a peer's, savings would build up gradually, not arrive at once. Existing staff are grandfathered; the saving comes only as people (a) reach the new, often earlier, grade ceiling and stop gaining increments, and (b) are promoted or newly hired onto the lower structure. This shows how the annual saving ramps from near-zero towards the full steady-state figure. It is an illustrative synthetic-cohort scenario (equal cohorts at every career stage, everyone progressing through all grades), not a forecast.

How this works & caveats
• A steady-state Exeter workforce is represented as equal cohorts at every career stage. At the switch, everyone keeps their current salary (no cut); future increments and promotions follow the target structure; leavers are replaced by new hires on the target structure.
• Saving in a given year = what the workforce would have cost continuing on Exeter's structure, minus what it costs with grandfathering + the target structure going forward. Year 1 is near zero (everyone grandfathered); it then climbs quickly — most of the saving lands within ~5 years — because the dominant mechanism is new promotions and hires arriving on the lower structure, which recurs every few years, rather than slow retirement turnover. A small transitional overshoot can occur (grandfathered staff who topped out early are briefly cheaper than steady-state hires) before it settles. The small hump above the dashed line, and its settling back down around the career-length mark (~20 yrs), are artefacts of the equal-cohort model (identical cohorts all retiring at the same age) — read the first ~10 years, where the decision-relevant ramp happens; the long-run figure is the steady-state line.
• Non-professorial only (the layer where structure bites). Professorial transition would add a slower, smaller component on top. Excludes attrition variation, redundancy/transition costs, and any negotiated protections beyond "no cut".
• Posts are equivalent average-salary posts (saving ÷ Exeter's average salary), not predicted job retention. Indicative, order-of-magnitude.
• Salaries above spine point 51 (local non-professorial spine extensions, which differ by institution) use each institution's own published values.
