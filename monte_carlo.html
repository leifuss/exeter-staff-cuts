How sure can we be? — the all-in figure as a range, not a number

The headline depends on four things nobody can pin down: how fast people are promoted, what share of topped-out staff hold a contribution point, how the HESA "Senior Lecturer / Associate Professor" total splits between Grades 2 and 3, and how often professors win a discretionary award. This runs the whole all-in model (lower grades + professoriate) 5,000 times, each time drawing those four from plausible ranges, and shows the spread of the result rather than one number. It uses the same engine as the all-in model; on-costs and pay year are fixed choices, not uncertainties. Whatever the draw, the population is the same: E&R and teaching-only academics plus the professoriate only — research-only and professional-services staff are not counted, so every number in the spread is a minimum estimate of the structural saving.

Reading & caveats
• "Equivalent posts" = annual saving ÷ Exeter's average cost per head — an equivalence for scale, not a prediction that this many jobs would be retained.
• Salaries above spine point 51 (local non-professorial spine extensions, which differ by institution) use each institution's own published values.

• The 5th–95th percentile is a sensitivity range under the selected assumptions — the input ranges are reasoned, not estimated, so it is not a full credible interval. The median is the central value.
• Seeded for reproducibility — the same settings give the same result; "Resample" draws a fresh set of 5,000.
• Ranges are reasoned, not measured (no sector data on pace dispersion, CP uptake or the true SL/AP split) — widen or narrow them as better Exeter data arrives. Covers the whole all-in model (lower grades + professoriate). Still outside the ranges: the HESA salary-band→grade mapping, transition behaviour, and local-point transcription — so this is a strong sensitivity test, not a complete accounting of uncertainty.
• Population = E&R + teaching-only academics and the professoriate only. Research-only staff (~780 full-time) and professional-services staff are excluded, and likely add savings — treat the whole distribution as a floor, not a midpoint.
